The Empowering Coach 

I am an empowering coach.

I have the right to be what I want-so I choose to be empowering.

I seek opportunity-not security.

I will not be limited in my possibilities

or focused on my past.  

I will take the high road, 

to be and to improve, 

to dream and to build, to transform fear into faith, and take risk in the pursuit of success. 

I prefer the challenges of life to the guaranteed existence, 

the thrill of learning and growing to the bondage of self-doubt.

I will not trade excellence for mediocrity

or shy away from obstacles.

It is my heritage and my choice 

to stand with assurance, 

to act on behalf of what is good for the cause of a healthy world.

As I coach I can leave a legacy

where others can enjoy the benefits

of what has been created.

I can face the world boldly and say:

"This has been created by an empowering coach, and that coach is me."

~Carl Casanova

Memories of Uganda

 

I step out of the plane

And I'm Home.

No more waiting, no more wondering; 

Just Living!

 

I'm Living in the moment.

Strange faces, out of place

And yet, they're all familiar to me.

 

The Lord's prepared me 

For this moment-

A moment I never knew.

 

I want to follow my dreams

Don't let me lose my vision.

"Father keep me healthy, Father keep me safe.

Let me stay here in this place."

 

Sitting under a mango tree 

Ears wide open...

"Speak to me.

Tell me why this feels so right."

Let me practice patience

Let me do what's right.

 

A doubly-large bee is in the cho with me

People laugh when I speak the language.

I can't stop saying period and there's full stops after every word.

 

I almost got run over, I didn't hear the beep.

Eye brows yes and unspoken communication.

Culture Shock!

 

The Lord has brought me here, I'll persevere.

I finally got to Africa-

Do I really have to leave?

 

Don't let me lose my vision

I'm bending on my knees.

Let me see things your way;

Grant me self-control.

 

Thanks for keeping me healthy.

Thanks for keeping me safe.

Lord you are so faithful...

Faithful to you I will be!

Lessons Learned: 

The Lord woke me up at 3:30.  I hoped he would wake me up again at 4:00 so feeling tired, I closed my eyes and fell back asleep.  The next hour and a half felt like only minutes as God revealed the condition of my heart through a dream.  

Matt and I were driving and Matt was in the back seat.  He was in a state of being drunk like.  We arrived at the location where he would be working with youth through the group mentoring for boys that he does.  I told him he shouldn't go and explained the shift in his personality I had seen in him.  He said he didn't see it.  I begged that he talk to the head of the program and he said he didn't see the change.  Feeling I needed to do something about this, I left the baby up the hill and trudged up the hill.  When I was approached and asked if I needed help, I persevered trudging up a steep hill.  When I made it to Matt, he apologized to me and I responded by slapping him.  I told him he shouldn't do that.  Walking off, I heard him ask someone what he should do to make things right and I was really mad because the advice was to bring home something.  He went off to apply this advice and I just got mad.  I became frightened and worried.  I rolled through a stop-light with a police officer behind me and he let me go on.  I became more angry and out of control.  I learned I need to stop trying to fix people and pray for them.  This is a lesson I continue to learn.
